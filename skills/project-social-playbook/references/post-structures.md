# Post Structures

Use these structures instead of inventing vague launch prose.

## P1. Problem -> Cost -> Wedge

Format:

1. Name the painful workflow or market failure.
2. Show the real cost.
3. Introduce the product wedge.
4. End with a specific CTA.

Best for:

- Workflow Wedge
- Market Narrative

## P2. Demo Proof

Format:

1. One-line claim.
2. Screenshot / clip / proof detail.
3. Why the proof matters.
4. CTA to try or react.

Best for:

- Consumer Magnet
- Workflow Wedge
- Protocol Breakthrough

## P3. Why Now

Format:

1. Category trend or ecosystem shift.
2. Why the old behavior no longer fits.
3. Why this project exists now.

Best for:

- Market Narrative
- Ecosystem Utility

## P4. Before / After

Format:

1. Before: current workflow.
2. After: new workflow.
3. What changed for the user.

Best for:

- Workflow Wedge
- Consumer Magnet

## P5. Belief Thread

Format:

1. A sharp opening belief.
2. Three supporting points.
3. Tie belief back to the product.

Best for:

- Protocol Breakthrough
- Market Narrative

## P6. Community Invitation

Format:

1. What is being built.
2. What kind of user or builder should join.
3. Exactly what response is wanted.

Best for:

- all archetypes

## P7. Reply / Quote Insertion

Format:

1. Agree or disagree quickly.
2. Add one concrete observation.
3. Connect lightly to the project only if earned.

Best for:

- engagement work

## P8. Objection Neutralizer

Format:

1. Name the skeptical objection.
2. Acknowledge why it exists.
3. Show how this product differs.

Best for:

- Protocol Breakthrough
- Market Narrative
