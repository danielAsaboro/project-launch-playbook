# Ecosystem Graph

Use this file to recommend communities, events, and reply targets. Prefer aligned rooms over the biggest room in crypto.

## Always-relevant routes

- Colosseum builder channels and demo-day adjacent rooms
- Superteam regional communities
- Solana developer communities
- ecosystem product/demo threads during hackathon season

## Community buckets

### Builder / infra

- Superteam regional Telegrams and Discords
- Anchor and Solana developer circles
- local hackathon coworking groups

### Consumer / culture

- consumer-app builders in the Solana ecosystem
- creator and community operators who already run onchain flows
- regional communities with strong event culture

### DeFi / trading

- trader and market-structure accounts
- protocol founder and research circles
- Spaces where strategy, execution quality, or product design get debated

## Event / AMA guidance

Prioritize:

- demo-driven Spaces
- founder AMAs where the host asks product questions
- community calls with live Q&A
- regional builder sessions during hackathon season

Avoid:

- generic “drop your project below” threads as the only distribution plan
- rooms where the audience has no overlap with your user

## Reply target heuristics

Choose 5 to 10 accounts that satisfy at least one of these:

- talks to the project’s actual users
- shapes the category narrative
- hosts relevant demo or community conversations
- would plausibly respect a thoughtful teardown or reply

Reply modes:

- add a concrete example
- sharpen the problem statement
- offer a contrarian edge with evidence
- share a screenshot or clip only when it directly fits the thread

## Regional seeding

If the project’s first users are likely to be local or community-led, seed there before optimizing for large-account attention.

Examples:

- campus product → regional student/operator communities
- devtool → builder Telegrams / Discords
- consumer app → creator / community-manager pockets
- trading product → trader/research circles
